Dealing with Hooch

Hooch is a dark liquid that forms on top of neglected starter. You can stir it in for a more sour flavor, but I prefer to pour it off for a milder taste.

Step 4

Shaping & Cold Prove

1

Pre-Shape

  • Tip dough onto a well-floured surface
  • Gently shape into a square
  • Be careful not to burst any air bubbles - they create the lovely open crumb!
  • Fold in sides and roll the dough up into a ball
2

Build Tension

Push and pull the dough ball in a series of motions to build surface tension. This creates a taut outer "skin" that helps the bread rise properly during baking.

Add more flour to your surface if the dough is sticking.

3

Into the Banneton

  • When the ball looks smooth and taut, pick it up carefully
  • Place it upside down in a well-floured banneton basket
  • Don't have a banneton? Flour a tea towel generously and line a bowl with it
4

Cold Prove in Refrigerator

‚è∞ Cooling is Critical!

Wait at least 1 hour before cutting

Ideally, wait until the bread is completely cool. If you cut it when it's too hot, the interior will be gummy and the bread will lose its shape as it deflates.

Exception: If you're eating the whole loaf in one sitting, go ahead and enjoy it warm!

üî™ Cutting Technique

Cut from the middle of the loaf, not the end. This keeps both cut surfaces together, preventing the bread from drying out quickly.

Storage Tips

  • Counter: Store cut-side down on a cutting board for 2-3 days
  • Bread box: Keeps crust crispy while maintaining interior moisture
  • Freezer: Slice before freezing for easy toast - lasts up to 3 months
  • Avoid: Plastic bags make the crust soft and chewy
