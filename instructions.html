Introduction

How to Make Sourdough Bread

Follow this step-by-step guide to create delicious homemade sourdough bread. Scroll down to progress through each stage of the baking process.

üìã What You'll Need

  • Active sourdough starter (50g maintained)
  • Bread flour (white or wholemeal)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Banneton basket or bowl with tea towel
  • Dutch oven
  • Baking paper
Step 1

Starter Maintenance

Basic Setup

Jar: 200g (empty without lid)

Starter amount: 50g

Maintenance Schedule

  • Keep starter in the fridge
  • Feed every three weeks (approximately)
  • Starter is "starving" when there's a layer of black water on top (called hooch)

Dealing with Hooch

You can stir the hooch into the starter for a more sour taste, but I prefer to tip it away.

Feeding Instructions

Feeding ratio: 1:1:1 (use white bread flour)

  • Don't keep more than 50g for a maintenance feed
  • Tip any excess in the bin (not down the sink)
  • Or save it and bake with it!
Step 2

Preparing Starter for Baking

1Big Feed

Take starter out of the fridge and do a big feed.

Feed Ratio: 1:3:3

  • 30g starter
  • 90g water
  • 90g bread flour

2Wait for Doubling

  • Leave until starter has doubled in size
  • Takes 4-10 hours (depending on temperature)
  • Keep lid loose so starter has enough oxygen

3Mix Your Dough

When starter has doubled in size, mix together:

Dough Ingredients

  • 100g starter (active)
  • 300g water
  • 450g bread flour (white or wholemeal as you fancy)
  • 10g salt
Step 3

Mixing & Proving

1Initial Mix

  • Mix ingredients together
  • Optional: put 40g of mixture in a small jar (to track proving)
  • Do not over mix - mixture should be well combined but shaggy in texture
  • Cover with tea towel/hair cap/cling film
  • Leave in warm place for 30 mins

2Stretch and Fold

  • After 30 mins: do 4x stretch and fold
  • If adding seeds or filling, do it during stretch and fold
  • Cover and leave for another 30 mins
  • Do 3 more rounds of stretch and fold

3Bulk Fermentation

  • Cover and leave for 4-10 hours
  • When your cup is full, your dough is proved

Tip: The small jar of dough acts as a visual indicator - when it doubles, your main dough is ready!

Step 4

Shaping & Cold Prove

1Shape the Dough

  • Tip dough onto a well-floured surface
  • Shape into a square
  • Be careful not to burst any air bubbles
  • Fold in sides and roll the dough up into a ball

2Build Tension

  • Complete a series of pushing and pulling the dough ball to build tension
  • Add more flour to surface if needed

3Into the Banneton

  • When the ball looks good, pick it up
  • Place it upside down in a well-floured banneton basket
  • Don't have a banneton? Flour a tea towel and put it in a bowl

4Cold Prove

Cover and put in the fridge to cold prove

Time range: 10 hours - 3 days

This develops flavor and makes scoring easier!

Step 5

Baking

1Preheat Dutch Oven

  • Put dutch oven in the oven for 20-30 mins
  • Set to high heat (around 200¬∞C)

2Prepare to Bake

  • After 30 mins, place dough on baking paper
  • Score the top (scissors work, but a scoring knife is ideal)

3Bake with Lid On

Put dough in dutch oven

Bake with lid on: 25-30 mins

4Bake with Lid Off

Bake with lid off: 25-30 mins

This creates that beautiful golden crust!

‚è∞ Important: Cooling Time

Wait at least 1 hour before cutting (ideally until bread is cool)

If you cut it when it's too hot, the bread will lose shape and be gummy in texture.

Exception: If you'll eat the whole loaf in one go, go wild and eat it warm!

Final Tips

Serving & Storage

üî™ Cutting Your Bread

Pro tip: Cut from the middle of the loaf, not the end

This helps keep the bread fresh longer!
